My Role
As a Service and Product Designer on this effort, I am leading its evolution by being responsible for the experience strategy and creating the design system. I guide the design and engineering work, produce all major deliverables, and present them to the leadership team. I am working alongside a UX designer as well as two engineers.
The Design Challenge

Empowering strategic needs and opportunities

We were asked to create a collection of repeatable components to build our products. However, a collection alone will not fix bad product design and development practices.

With this very clear in our minds, our goal was to create an ever-evolving system that would allow us to build higher quality products more efficiently.

The Method

What was our design system maturity model?

To understand the current state of our products, I created a comprehensive audit of the user interface elements that are visible in our products.

The design exercise reflected a range of products where not only the components are inconsistent, but the user experience varies drastically from one to the other.

The Discovery

What does "having" a design system mean for our teams?

During this phase we wanted to familiarise ourselves more deeply with the way we currently execute and deliver our work.

I scheduled several design activities to understand their thoughts and see where we can make a difference so that the service is user-friendly, competitive, and relevant to our teammates.

A design system is a series of components that can be reused in different combinations. Design systems allow you to manage design at scale.
UX Director
The central GIT repository of code-based components that engineers use to develop a product.
Lead Engineer
A set of guidelines and documentation that empower disparate teams to create, maintain, and extend product development efficiently and consistently.
Senior Director of Product
A centralized resource that contains documentation of the system and information on design principles, guides on topics like content, design, and accessibility.
UX Designer

The research confirmed that our teammates recognize the design system as a service to people. If we want broad adoption, we needed a strong understanding of designers' and developers' workflows.

With that in mind, I opted to map how we work and put together a design system process that aligns with our six-week cycles without interrupting or affecting our current velocity.

Also, if our design system is to mature and evolve, it will need governance guidelines, so the process is built into the organization.

Reframing the Problem

Optimizing a plan to maximize adoption reach

Now that we understand what is important to our teammates and the business, I am leading a small team to build a system to lead us to a measurable increase in productivity by improving process efficiencies across business units and the company.

The goal is to create a service that promotes the following factors.

Task Velocity

Increased team morale & retention through higher impact work and improved collaboration.

Feature Releases

Decreased time to market through process efficiencies and higher productivity.

Code Quality

Decreased cost of development through fewer bugs and increased scalability.

User Experience

Increased brand equity and usability through visual coherence and baked-in accessibility.

The Vision

A systematic approach to design foundations

How our members feel and what affects their feelings—is essential if we want to provide great experiences.

Members can’t have an engaging, delightful, inspiring experience unless it’s well-designed and thought out. That’s where we’re focused in terms of design.

We created a soft, warm, and healing style.

Designing the System

A design language with empathy

Emotional Design

We have added an emotional design layer to the system to have an engaging, delightful, inspiring experience.

Source: The Art of Emotion — Norman’s 3 Levels of Emotional Design
Design for Coherence

Coherence aims for clarity. Therefore we are:

• Building coherence will enable us to create a better, more resilient and flexible system.

• Establishing better foundations that are clear, easy to understand, and use.

• Setting up a process that ensures that we are working consistently and efficiently.

Source: Designing for Coherence not Consistency
